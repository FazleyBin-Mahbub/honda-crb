Frequently Asked Questions

If your looking for cycling not motorcycling

For that first you must understand the types of bicycles

  • Road Cycle
  • Mountain Cycle
  • Hybrid Cycle
Choice of above will depend on the purpose of your cycling interest.

Like if you wish to use cycle for office commute you choose road cycles which suits for plain roads, pavements most of the leveled surface. Or if you like adventure climbing mountains or off road drive mountain cycles will be apt. Or your clueless what to choose try hybrid cycles it will fit almost all the use case.

If your wondering about prerequisites for cycling then

  • Helmet
  • Front and rear lights ( reflector aleast)
  • Cycling Gloves
  • Cycle Lock
  • Shoes
  • Water bottle holder with bottle
  • Waist pouch or Bike Bag
  • Check brakes and Tyre pressure
  • GPS enabled devices (Smartphone with good Cycling app Runtatic)
  • You can wear most anything to ride a bike. But, clothes especially designed for riding are a wonder.

Bear in mind that if the helmet did its job most people would tell you that they did not even hit their head, or did not hit their head that hard. And the thin shells on most helmets now tend to hide any dents in the foam. But if you can see marks on the shell or measure any foam crush at all, replace the helmet.

Before going on a long ride, take your bike around the block and make sure it is safe to ride. First of all, your tires are going to be deflated, it is normal after long periods of time on storage. Inflate them close to the recommended level, you can find that in the wall of the tire. Make sure your brakes work. First do it off your bike, make sure the brake pads make contact with the rim or the rotor appropriately. Then do it with while riding your bike around the block. Make sure you test it with enough speed, but do it safely

When riding, always:
  • Go With the Traffic Flow. Ride on the right in the same direction as other vehicles. ...
  • Obey All Traffic Laws. A bicycle is a vehicle and you're a driver. ...
  • Yield to Traffic When Appropriate. ...
  • Be Predictable. ...
  • Stay Alert at All Times. ...
  • Look Before Turning. ...
  • Watch for Parked Cars.
